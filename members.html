Steps to Pervent LDL

Change Your Eating Habits

There is Multiple ways to change your LDL levels if they are high. One of the most simplest ways in doing so is chaning your eating habits. The type of fats you eat are a big contributing factor into how high or low your cholestrol is. The fats to stay away from are Saturated and Trans fats, these two fats will raise your LDL insteadd of lowering it. The fat that you should eat is Unsatruared fats aka "Healthy Fats". What are Healthy Fats? Omega-3 fatty acids don't affect LDL cholesterol. They have other heart-healthy benefits, including reducing blood pressure. Foods with omega-3 fatty acids include salmon, mackerel, walnuts and flaxseeds.

Exerise!

Physical Activity can improve your cholesterol. Moderate physical activity can raise your HDL levels, up to 30 minutes of physical activity for five times a week. Don't have 30 minutes? It's okay even small amounts of physical activity on a regular basis can help! Vigorous aerobic activity for 20 minutes three times a week.

If You Smoke, Stop It!

Quitting smoking improves your HDL cholesterol level and is better for your overall health anyways. The benefits occur of quitting somking happen quickly, within 20 minutes your blood pressure and heart rate recover from the cigarette-induced spike. In three months your blood circulation and lung function begin to improve and within a year of quitting, your risk of heart disease is half that of a smoker

Drink Alcohol, But Limit Yourself

Monitoring your intake of alcohol had been linked to having higher levels of HDL, but even if you don't drink, it okay because the benefits of drinking alcohol is still not that high to wear you would need to drink anyways. But if you do and don't want to give that up it's advised to have one drink a day for women of all ages and for males that are 65 or older but for males 65 and younger two drinks per day is enough. Don't overstep the limits because too much alcohol can lead to high blood pressure, heart failure and stroke.
